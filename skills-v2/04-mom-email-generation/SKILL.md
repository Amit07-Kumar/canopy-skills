# MoM Email Generation (Professional Launch-Mail Style)

## When to use this skill

- The auto-sent MoM email is going out as a generic 2-line summary and
  recipients can't make sense of what the meeting decided.
- The subject is "AI daily scrum summary May 16 2026" — generic, unrelatable.
- A PM or stakeholder asked: "what was the decision, who owns what, when's
  the deadline, and what's next?" and the current email doesn't answer.

## How to apply

Two parallel generators keep the UI preview and the auto-dispatched copy in
lockstep:

### 1. Server-side (auto-dispatch)

`meeting-master/backend/api.py`:

- `_build_professional_mom_subject(meeting)` →
  `MoM • <Title> • <DD MMM YYYY>` (e.g. `MoM • Q3 Pricing Review • 16 May 2026`).
- `_build_professional_mom_body(meeting, recipients)` → full Markdown body
  with sections (see schema below).
- Both called from `_auto_dispatch_meeting_outputs` BEFORE the google_tool_event
  webhook is hit, so the email that lands in inboxes already has the rich body.
- The original AISummarization body (if any) is preserved under `mail.body_native`.

### 2. Frontend (editable preview)

`meeting-master/frontend/app.js`:

- `App.buildMomSubject(meeting)` and `App.buildMomBody(meeting, recipients)`
  mirror the backend builders (same section order, same fields).
- Called from `displayResults` so every time a meeting completes the user
  sees the full draft in the email editor.
- Default view is **edit mode** (not preview-locked) — full select-all, paste,
  edit allowed. See [[11-frontend-ux-patterns]].

## Standard MoM section order

```markdown
# Minutes of Meeting — <Title>

**Date:** <Month DD, YYYY>
**To:** <comma-separated recipients>
**Topic:** <topic from AISummarization MOM>  (optional)

---

## Attendees
- **<Name>** (<email>)         # one bullet per attendee, both name and email when known
- **<Name>**
- <email>                      # email-only fallback

---

## Discussion
<discussion_summary from AISummarization>

## Decisions  (optional, only when present)
<decisions text>

---

## Action Items
| # | Action Item | Owner | Category | Priority | Due |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **<task title>** | <assignee or _TBD_> | <Ops|Development|...> | <HIGH|MEDIUM|LOW> | <YYYY-MM-DD> |
| 2 | ...                                                                                  |

---

## Calendar Holds
1. **<event_title>** — <Month DD, YYYY at HH:MM AM/PM>
   <description>

2. ...

---

## Execution Snapshot
- **Execution Health Index:** <N> / 100
- **Context Completeness:** <N>%
- **Action Leakage:** <N>%
- **Ownership Coverage:** <N>%
- **Auto-dispatch:** Email + Calendar sent automatically | Pending

---

## Next Steps
1. Confirm action item owners and deadlines by replying to this email.
2. Calendar holds above will be sent as Google Calendar invites — accept or reschedule.
3. If a BRD is needed for this initiative, click **Generate BRD** in the workspace.

---

_Auto-generated by Canopy Meeting Workspace._
```

## What the user can edit

Everything below the subject line. The `email-editor` div is `contenteditable`
with `user-select: text`, no preview lock. Toggling **Preview** re-renders
the Markdown as styled HTML for at-a-glance review; toggling **Edit Markdown**
puts the raw Markdown back so the user can freely modify.

## Subject contract

Format: `MoM • <Title> • <DD MMM YYYY>`

Reasoning: short, deterministic, scannable in Gmail's truncated subject line.
"MoM" prefix lets recipients filter / search past meetings. Title comes from
`meeting.title` (set by the user during upload) or fallback to the AI-detected
topic. Date is the meeting's `created_at`, not the LLM's hallucinated date.

Never use the Groq raw output as the subject — it sometimes emits "AI daily
scrum summary" which is unrelatable.

## Verification

```powershell
# Trigger a fresh meeting and inspect the dispatched mail
python e2e_audio_validation.py
# Then in the meeting record, check:
#   mail.subject  → "MoM • <Title> • <Date>"
#   mail.body     → starts with "# Minutes of Meeting"
#   mail.body     → contains "## Action Items", "## Calendar Holds", "## Execution Snapshot"
```
